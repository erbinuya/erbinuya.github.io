How to Deal with Unemployment?

Ever since I was young, I have always wondered why there are many poor beggars on the streets, asking for alms. Why were they in that position in the first place? Where are their families, and how come they aren’t there for them? I then realized that they were really unfortunate to be affected by the problem of unemployment. Unemployment refers to the state of a person not being able to find or have a job, whether they want to work or not. From small countries like Maldives, to elaborate archipelagos like the Philippines and Indonesia, up until gigantic ones with humungous population like that of China and the United States of America, unemployment plays a huge role in affecting the economic prosperity of a nation. It can lead to many different sorts of problems, such as lower tax revenues and increased welfare spending. These revenues are being used for investments for education, services, as well as to finance the government and maintain public works. At the same time, the government will be the one taking care of these people, providing them with the necessities, which would result to debt as well as poverty. With this in mind, we should have a way in order to fix this problem and prevent others from experiencing it. According to Chitra Reddy on her article, ”What is Unemployment? Its Causes, Effects, and Solutions”, the workforce of any country makes up a large part of its population. In order to be able to provide the needs of the people, all countries should ensure job availability so that all people will be able to get the chance to have work. However, is there another way to solve the problem that we face?

There are a lot of people that are unemployed, which are not being able to contribute to the country. We can consider them as the “burdens” and every time a person is unemployed, we can say that there is a chance that person will likely to become a burden in the future. What must we do to lessen the number of the unemployed present in the country? First, we must know which people are unemployed. We can set up a totalitarian government which is a type of government that tracks down every action an individual does. This is similar to the type of government that many historical figures established in order to maximize the capacity of their power, such as Joseph Stalin of the Soviet Union, as well as Adolf Hitler of the Nazi Germany. Under totalitarian rule, many of the social institutions and organizations will be discouraged, and this in turn will absorb the people into a single, unified society obedient to the rules and regulations implemented by the government. Even though it limits the amount of freedom one will have, such as the ability to move and act freely in every aspect of your life, it is a fair trade in order to improve the economy of the nation, which will then be able to help improve the lives of every other citizens in the country. Now that we are able to track people, we can now identify which are unemployed. We can give a certain amount of time for those who are currently unemployed to look for a job, let’s say the time limit is about 150 days, and if they will not be able to have one during this specific timeframe, they may request for an extension for about 30 days. However, we must also consider that some of the unemployed couldn’t find jobs due to certain factors, such as more specialized job descriptions and maybe even because of poverty. So, in order to give them an opportunity, we can increase the job availability present in the country and also increase the amount of job options so that they would be able to choose. And if they are still unable to find a decent job at least, this is where the consequences take in effect.

These consequences may include severe torture, mutilation, imprisonment, destruction of properties, and worse, death. Why do we have these consequences or punishments? These will serve as example for those who are unwilling to do work for the country, and also to those who given more choices to choose from. You were already provided with an ample amount of time to find at least a decent job, and the job options that you will choose from has already been increased, so you should have been able to comply. These punishments will be publicly executed, so that the public eye will be able see how big of a problem it is. If the people were to see these harsh punishments enacted upon the jobless, if they were to see blood pouring out where the fingernails resided, would they sit by and stay unemployed? Either you find work, or face the consequences. This way, we will be sure to eliminate any useless citizens who doesn’t have capability to at least provide the minimum service they can to the government, especially to the country. More and more citizens will be able to work and serve the government, therefore the amount of “burden” will lessen.

Unemployment affects us all in many different ways. It can affect us personally, socially, and it can also affect the whole country as a whole. With unemployment rates increasing as time goes by, the economy of the country is slowly being affected. The ones who will be providing the facilities as well as necessities for the unemployed will be the government, and because of that, the burden of debt of the government will definitely increase. Another effect of unemployment is lower income, which might lead to poor living conditions and health risks to the people. All in all, unemployment affects each and everyone of us, and it is currently present in almost all countries in the world. That is why all of us should be trying to look for the solutions to this problem, to find cure for the cancer that has already enveloped the most parts of the world.
